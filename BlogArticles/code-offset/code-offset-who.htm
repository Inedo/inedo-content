The Faces behind Inedo's Code Offsets

When we first thought of relaunching Code Offsets, the idea of choosing important figures from the history of programming and computing seemed like a fairly straightforward task. As it turned out, there were a few more twists and turns than anticipated.

Do I just use programmers? What about the engineers of the first computers? What about the mathematicians whose theories explained the very idea of computing? What about the business people, politicians, teachers, scientists, and researchers who pushed those devices to their limits, and found new and effecting uses for them?

Pretty quickly, I had a list long enough to make hundreds of different Code Offset bills. Paring it down was one of the most laborious parts of whole project. Doubtlessly, we had to cut out one of your favorites, and we certainly had to drop a few of ours.

As that short list developed, we tried to memorialize the people we could most directly link with the development of modern programing and computing as we could. To a person, they straddle the lines of the fields we mentioned above, mathematician engineers, scientist financiers, and even an Admiral programmer.

There certainly will be more Code Offsets incoming, so if you have opinions, please let me know! Feel free to email me at proach@indeo.com, and maybe we can chat about the second editions of our Code Offsets.

So follow along as we post our nine part series, and discover why we picked each person, as well as a few little tidbits of computing history!
