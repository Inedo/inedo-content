Jeremy: One of the first things we talk about with any client is having the concept of having small wins. To use a sports analogy; first downs make touchdowns.

...small incremental deliveries have more flexibility which helps mitigate risk.

Speaking of job satisfaction... Why did you decide to focus on consulting rather than become an in-house dev some place?

Jeremy: Really, we formed Sycorr in 2010 because consulting can be incredibly gratifying and it helps us to stay fresh and up-to-date.

There is something about working with a variety of organizations that is alluring. New challenging problems being solved with a dash of past experience is extremely rewarding for us.

I think you've probably just given part of the answer, but why did you form a small company rather than join a large consulting firm?

Jeremy: Simply put, we wanted to control who we work with, we wanted to work with extremely talented and passionate developers. When you have large teams, you have a few heroes, but when you have a small business – everyone needs to be excellent to survive.

We created Sycorr to help banks and credit unions leverage technology in ways that the industry is not currently. Regulatory concerns has stifled innovation and we wanted to do something about that. We wanted to break through the red tape and use our abilities to show financial institutions how technology can be a true asset.

So focusing on the financial sector is obviously part of it, but do you have a speciality?

Jeremy: Our clients would probably say we reduce operational overhead by focusing on automation of business problems.

Due to the industry we typically serve, our team is sensitive to concepts around separation of concerns, limited downtime releases, logging/auditing, working with legacy systems, and automation of test labs.

If that's what your clients say, what do you say?

Jeremy: Bringing our DevOps mentality and financial sector knowledge to the table helps our clients by removing the slow, error prone, and manual processes that are easily solved using proven tools.

Yes, there are significant challenges because of regulations, and sensitive or protected data, but they are challenges that can be met and overcome.

It sounds like you're really passionate about this, any favorite success stories?

Great story! Since time changes only happen twice a year, what's something that you see much more commonly... in a bad way?

Jeremy: Far too many manual processes that take too much time and cause inconsistent results. Just mapping the processes is often one of the first things we have to tackle.

Yeah, lack of consistent results of any kind isn't what you want to see! What's the most over-used buzz word you're coming across?

Jeremy: Disruptive. Saying you are "disruptive" probably means you are entering into an established market with competitors larger than you are. You aren't going to beat them at their game; that's just silly.

Innovate! Be so innovative that you change how people work, act, or think; that's shaking things up, that's how you achieve success!
